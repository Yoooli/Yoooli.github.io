Uluru is one of Australia's famous landmarks, and is here mapped as a polygon using leaflet and google earth. It is of great cultural significance to the indigenous local community, the Anangu people. For visitors, it is an impressive and beautiful tourist attraction. Also known as Ayers Rock, Uluru is a sandstone rock formation in the Northern Territory in Australia. It sticks out and up from the flat plains surrounding it, reaching a height of 348m from the ground. Geologically it is a feature called an inselberg, or 'island mountain'. It is an erosional feature, remaining when its surroundings eroded because of its relative resistance to weathering.
